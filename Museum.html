The Prado Museum, named in 1868 after the exile of Isabella II, who expanded his collection to include paintings from the Royal Palace and Escorial. Spain's Prado Museum and Madrid's Art Gallery contain the world's richest and most complete collection of Spanish paintings, as well as masterpieces of European painting. Museo Thyssen-Bornemisza

  • Monday (12 to 4PM) & Tuesday-Sunday (10AM to 7PM)
  • Price: €12
  • P.º del Prado, 8, 28014 Madrid, Spain

The Thyssen-Bornemisza collection excels particularly, since their collections are unique and not represented in other Spanish museums. The Thyssen museums have an ample collection of paintings of the 14th century in Italy from painters such as Trecento, Duccio di Buoninsegna, and works by early Dutch artists, such as Jan Van Eyck's Diptych of Annunciation. Furthermore, it has an exquisite collection of 15th-century portraits, including the famous portrait of Giovanna Tornabuoni by Ghirlandaio. Finally, it also envolpes the works of Dürer, Caravaggio, Rubens, Frans Hals and Canaletto tracing the trajectory of European art between the 16th and 18th centuries.

Museo Nacional de Ciencias Naturales

  • Monday (closed) / Tuesday-Saturday (10AM to 5PM) / Sunday (10AM to 8PM)
  • Price. General at €7 and Reduced at €3.50 (students, unemployed and children from 4 to 14)
  • C. de José Gutiérrez Abascal, 2, 28006 Madrid, Spain

The National Museum of Natural Sciences maintains and makes available to the public an important collection of specimens to provide a more complete picture of the diversity of the natural world. This information is used to protect the environment and share with communities to raise people's awareness of science and natural resources. In 1771, Charles III established the Royal Natural History Cabinet, which later became the National Museum of Natural Sciences. It has been renamed several times throughout history. The Royal Natural History Cabinet started with the magnificent collection and library of Pedro Franco Davila, a Spanish businessman from Guayaquil, who donated it to the Spanish royal family.

Museo Sorolla

  • P.º del Gral. Martínez Campos, 37, 28010 Madrid, Spain

The Sorolla Museum preserves the true environment of the home and studio of the painter Joacn Sorolla y Bastida (Valencia, 1863; Cercedilla, 1923). Here is the largest collection of his work, vivid paintings that radiate light and energy. It is one of the most complete and well-preserved artist residences in Europe. The gardens he designed are a great getaway in the city.
